# Summary of "The Tragedy of Taiwan" Series (1946)

This document provides a summary and analysis of the investigative series by Harlow M. Church for international readers.

## Overview
In April 1946, Harlow M. Church exposed the deteriorating conditions in Taiwan following the transition from Japanese colonial rule to the Nationalist (KMT) administration.

## Part-by-Part Breakdown

- **Part 1 (Apr 15):** Focuses on administrative chaos and the "Out of the frying pan into the fire" sentiment. Highlights the exclusion of local Taiwanese from the government and the rise of criminal gangs.
- **Part 2 (Apr 16):** Documents the "extortion rackets" targeting repatriating Japanese civilians. Reports on the seizure of property and illegal transportation fees charged by corrupt officials.
- **Part 3 (Apr 17):** Analyzes "The Big Squeeze." The government-controlled monopoly over essential goods (rice, sugar, coal) led to severe hyperinflation and artificial food shortages.
- **Part 4 (Apr 18):** Observes industrial paralysis. Factories remained idle due to looting of parts and mismanagement, while agricultural yield plummeted due to lack of fertilizers.

## Historical Significance
These reports documented the root causes of the widespread public anger that eventually exploded into the **February 28 Incident (2-28)** in 1947. Church’s observations align with the popular Taiwanese saying of the time: *"The dogs (Japanese) went, but the pigs (KMT) came."*
