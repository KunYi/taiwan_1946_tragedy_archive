# Comparative Analysis: Taiwan Under Transition (1946)

| Category | Late Japanese Era / Ideal Expectation | Post-WWII Reality (April 1946) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Administration** | Relative institutional stability despite colonial nature. | **Systemic Failure:** Conflicting orders; exclusion of local elite. |
| **Language** | Widespread use of Japanese and local dialects. | **Barriers:** Forced Mandarin; 65% of people could not understand decrees. |
| **Rice Industry** | "Granary of the Empire" with high export capacity. | **Food Crisis:** Black market spikes (Prices rose from 2 to 20 Yen). |
| **Industry** | Modernized factories; Cement: 12,000 barrels/day. | **Paralysis:** Looting of parts; Cement: 1,000 barrels/day. |
| **Resources** | Consistent supply of fertilizers to farmers. | **Exploitation:** Monopolies diverted resources for private profit. |
| **Security** | Strict legal enforcement. | **Decay:** Rising kidnappings; gangs colluding with officials. |
| **Sentiment** | Eager for liberation; initial joy. | **Disillusionment:** "Dogs go, Pigs come" sentiment. |

---
*Source: Reconstructed from Harlow M. Church's series in The Pittsburgh Press (1946).*
