# Historical Context: Harlow M. Church and The Pittsburgh Press

## About the Author
**Harlow M. Church** was an American correspondent known for his investigative journalism in East Asia following World War II. His reporting from Taiwan in 1946 is among the few detailed Western accounts of the "Retrocession" period before the curtain of censorship tightened.

## The Pittsburgh Press
The *Pittsburgh Press* was a major afternoon newspaper in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. During the mid-20th century, it was a prominent voice in American journalism (now part of Block Communications). Its international reporting often provided candid views on the post-war reconstruction of Asia.

## The "Dogs go, Pigs come" Metaphor: A Cross-Reference
While Harlow M. Church used the Western idiom **"Out of the frying pan into the fire"** to describe the situation in Taiwan, his reports align perfectly with the famous local saying: **"Dogs go, Pigs come"** (狗去豬來).

This metaphor was later immortalized in Western literature by **George H. Kerr** (a U.S. Vice-Consul in Taipei from 1945–1947) in his seminal work:
> *"The Japanese were dogs; they barked and bit, but they kept the house. The Chinese are pigs; they only eat."* — **George H. Kerr, *Formosa Betrayed* (1965)**

The fact that both a journalist (Church) and a diplomat (Kerr) recorded identical grievances regarding the "Big Squeeze" and systemic corruption during the same period provides powerful cross-verification of the historical truth.

## Why These Articles Matter
These articles are a "missing link" for many historians. While internal Chinese/Taiwanese records from this era were often sanitized or destroyed during the subsequent Martial Law period, these Western reports provide:
1. **Raw Economic Data:** Contemporary prices of rice and specific industrial output numbers.
2. **Unfiltered Public Sentiment:** Direct quotes from frustrated Taiwanese citizens expressing disillusionment.
3. **External Verification:** Independent, third-party confirmation of the socio-economic collapse that served as the primary catalyst for the **February 28 Incident (2-28)** in 1947.

## Recommended Reading
- **Kerr, George H.** *Formosa Betrayed* (1965). (The definitive eyewitness account of the 2-28 era).
- **Ho, Samuel P. S.** *Economic Development of Taiwan, 1860-1970* (1978). (A scholarly analysis of Taiwan’s economic transition).
- **Phillips, Steven E.** *Between Assimilation and Independence: The Taiwanese Elite under Nationalist Chinese Rule, 1945-1950*.
